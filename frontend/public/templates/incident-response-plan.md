## The 5 Steps

### Step 1: Disconnect (but don't turn off)

- Unplug the network cable or turn off Wi-Fi on the affected device.
- **Do not shut down or restart the computer** — this can destroy evidence needed to understand what happened.

### Step 2: Call [IT CONTACT NAME]

- Phone: [IT CONTACT PHONE]
- Email: [IT CONTACT EMAIL]
- Backup: [BACKUP CONTACT NAME] at [BACKUP CONTACT PHONE]

Call immediately. Do not wait until the end of the day or "after this meeting."

### Step 3: Do not attempt to fix it yourself

- Do not delete suspicious files.
- Do not run antivirus scans unless told to.
- Do not forward the suspicious email to colleagues.
- Leave the device as-is until [IT CONTACT NAME] gives instructions.

### Step 4: Write down what happened

Fill in the incident log below while it's fresh in your memory. Even small details help.

### Step 5: Change passwords for affected accounts

- If you entered credentials anywhere suspicious, change those passwords immediately using [PASSWORD MANAGER NAME].
- Enable 2FA if it wasn't already on.
- If the same password was used elsewhere (it shouldn't be), change it there too.
