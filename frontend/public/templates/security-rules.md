## Purpose

These rules protect [YOUR COMPANY NAME], our customers, and our employees from cyber threats.

---

## Password Rules

- Use a unique password for every work account. Never reuse personal passwords.
- Passwords must be at least 12 characters long.
- Use our password manager: [PASSWORD MANAGER NAME, e.g., Bitwarden / 1Password].
- Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA/MFA) on all accounts that support it.
- Never share passwords by email, chat, or text message.
- Change your password immediately if you think it may have been compromised.

---

## Email and Phishing

- Do not click links or open attachments from unknown senders.
- Check the sender's email address carefully — look for misspellings or unusual domains.
- If an email asks you to enter your password, verify the request by contacting the sender directly (not by replying to the email).
- Report any suspicious email to [IT CONTACT NAME] at [IT CONTACT EMAIL/PHONE].
- When in doubt, ask before you click.

---

## Software and Updates

- Only install software approved by [IT CONTACT NAME / MANAGEMENT].
- Keep your computer and phone updated — install updates within [e.g., 48 hours] of notification.
- Do not use personal USB drives on work computers without approval.
- Use only company-approved cloud storage: [e.g., Google Drive, OneDrive, Dropbox].

---

## Reporting Incidents

If you think something is wrong — you clicked a bad link, lost a device, saw something unusual — report it immediately.

1. Call or message [IT CONTACT NAME] at [IT CONTACT EMAIL/PHONE].
2. Disconnect from the network if instructed to do so.
3. Write down what happened (what, when, how).

**You will not be punished for reporting a mistake.** Fast reporting protects everyone.
